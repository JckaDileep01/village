Electricity

Electricity comes and goes without warning. Long power cuts disturb education, healthcare, and daily life. Children study under dim lights, and households suffer in extreme heat and rain. In today‚Äôs world, darkness should not still define village life.

Water & Sanitation

Water decides our daily routine, our health, and our dignity. Many families still struggle for clean drinking water, waiting hours or walking long distances. Poor sanitation leads to illness, especially among children. Clean water and proper sanitation are not facilities ‚Äî they are basic rights.

Education Challenges

Our children want to learn, but the system fails them. Schools lack proper classrooms, learning materials, and digital facilities. Many students drop out early, not because of lack of talent, but because opportunities never reach them.

Healthcare & Emergency Support

When illness strikes, fear follows. There is no nearby hospital, no emergency ambulance, and no immediate medical support. In critical moments, villagers depend on distant towns, risking lives due to delays. Health should not be decided by distance.

Roads & Transport

Broken roads are not just inconvenience ‚Äî they are barriers to life. During rains, paths turn into mud, cutting the village off from schools, hospitals, and markets. Pregnant women, students, and elderly people suffer the most. A village without proper roads remains invisible to development.

Internet & Connectivity

The internet connects the world, but our village remains disconnected. Poor network and slow connectivity block access to online education, government services, and employment opportunities. In the digital age, lack of connectivity pushes the village further behind.

Housing & Street Lights

Many homes lack proper structure, safety, and protection during harsh weather. At night, poor or non-functional street lights create fear and insecurity, especially for women and elders. A safe home and a lit street are the foundation of a dignified life.

Women & Child Welfare

Women carry the heaviest burden ‚Äî managing homes, work, and children with limited support. Lack of awareness, safety measures, and skill opportunities restrict their growth. Children, the future of the village, suffer from malnutrition and poor facilities.

Employment & Livelihood

Most families depend on agriculture and daily wages, both uncertain and seasonal. When crops fail or work disappears, hunger enters homes quietly. The village needs skill training, local employment, and sustainable income sources.

Disaster Preparedness & Alerts

Heavy rains, floods, and natural challenges arrive without warning. There is no proper alert system, no preparedness, and no timely information. Villagers suffer losses simply because no one warned them in time.
