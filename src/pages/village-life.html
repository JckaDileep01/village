Village Life

Culture & Heritage

Our village culture is deeply rooted in tradition, unity, and simplicity. Elders pass down values through stories, customs, and daily practices that reflect respect for nature, family bonds, and community life. Traditional clothing, folk songs, and village gatherings still hold importance, keeping our heritage alive even as times change.

Temples & Festivals

Temples are the spiritual heart of the village and a place where people come together in faith and harmony. Festivals are celebrated with devotion, joy, and collective participation. During these occasions, the village comes alive with prayers, rituals, decorations, and cultural programs, strengthening unity and preserving age-old traditions.

School

The village school plays a crucial role in shaping the future of our children. It provides basic education and moral values, serving as the first step toward knowledge and growth. Despite limited resources, teachers work hard to guide students and inspire them to dream of better opportunities.

Anganwadi

The Anganwadi center supports early childhood care and development. It provides nutrition, basic education, and health awareness for young children, pregnant women, and mothers. This center plays an important role in improving child health, reducing malnutrition, and creating awareness about hygiene and well-being.

Daily Life & Occupations

Village life begins early with people engaged in agriculture, labor work, dairy farming, and small trades. Farming remains the main occupation, closely connected to seasons and nature. Life here is simple but hardworking, with people depending on mutual support, shared resources, and strong community relationships.
